Extractions & Root Canals

Simple, surgical, and root-tip extractions plus endodontic care — performed in-office or referred to a specialist depending on the tooth.

Teeth aren’t pulled — they’re carefully removed

Contrary to popular belief, teeth aren’t “pulled.” They’re removed by carefully and systematically applying controlled force and pressure in different directions until the tooth comes loose. We perform three types of extractions depending on the tooth and its condition.

Simple extractions are performed on teeth that are visible in the mouth, typically loosened with an elevator before being lifted out with forceps. Surgical extractions involve teeth that are compromised, broken, severely decayed, or have not fully erupted — sometimes requiring an incision or splitting the tooth into pieces for easier removal. Root tip extractions involve teeth that have broken at or below the gum line.

There are many reasons a tooth might need to be removed: severe decay, infection, gum disease, fracture, impaction, preparation for orthodontics, or teeth that simply can’t be restored with root canal treatment. Some extractions are performed here at Zent Family Dentistry, and others are referred to an oral surgeon — we’ll always recommend the path that’s right for your tooth.

  • Simple extractions
  • Surgical extractions
  • Root tip extractions
  • Post-op surgical instructions
  • Referrals to oral surgeons when needed
  • Replacement planning (implants, bridges, dentures)

Compassionate care when you need it most

Save Natural Teeth

Root canals preserve your natural tooth, maintaining your bite, jawbone health, and smile without the need for implants or bridges.

Pain Relief

These procedures eliminate the source of dental pain. Most patients feel immediate relief and are back to normal within days.

Efficient Treatment

Modern techniques mean most root canals are completed in a single visit, getting you out of discomfort quickly and comfortably.

Prevent Spread

Treating infection promptly prevents it from spreading to surrounding teeth or into the jawbone, protecting your overall oral health.
